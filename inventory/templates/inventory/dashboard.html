Projected inventory

Everything here is calculated when you open the dashboard from your live warehouse data — numbers are not hard-coded.

What you are seeing
  • Historical total units — end-of-day combined quantity across active items (same series as the stock trend card, with a shorter history window on the chart for clarity).
  • Projected units — model estimate for each future day; the dashed line starts after today.
  • Shaded band — upper and lower bounds from the model’s uncertainty interval (wider band = more uncertainty).
  • Today — vertical marker at the boundary between recorded history and the forecast.
Summary tiles
  • Projected inventory — headline figure (next-day or end-of-horizon style projection from the model output).
  • Forecast change — percentage move across the selected horizon; “No significant change” when the move is small.
  • Expected range — min–max from the confidence interval over the horizon.
  • Confidence — qualitative label derived from how tight that interval is.
Projection insight & metrics

The insight text summarises direction and magnitude. Model performance snapshot shows backtest errors (MAE, RMSE, MAPE) for a simple baseline versus the advanced model so you can judge fit on recent history.

Inventory stock trend

The line chart is built from your database each time you load the dashboard.

What the line shows

Each point is total units in stock at end of day (sum of quantities), for the last 7, 14, or 30 days depending on the selector.

Tiles
  • Current stock units — value on the latest day in the window.
  • Change over period — percentage change from the first to the last day in the window.
  • Daily points — number of days plotted (may be fewer than the range if data started recently).
Axis and comparison

The vertical scale is shared with the projected inventory chart so you can compare levels at a glance. X-axis labels use fixed spacing from “today” to keep dates readable.
